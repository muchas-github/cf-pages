What Is Landscape Bark?

As a simple definition landscape bark (also known as beauty bark) is a type of bark that’s specifically intended for use in landscaping projects. Similar to mulch in certain respects, landscape bark in Tacoma is often utilized in front yards, back yards, corporate landscaping, and to enhance garden spaces. However, there are some key differences between landscape bark and mulch (we’ll get to that in a bit). Typically, beauty bark in Tacoma is derived from coniferous, softwood trees like redwood, fir, spruce, and pine trees. Once it’s removed from the tree, the bark is then specially graded to make it ideal for landscaping.

Mulch vs. Landscape Bark in Tacoma

For homeowners and other types of property owners, choosing between decorative mulch and beauty bark in Tacoma relies on a few key pieces of information. First, it’s important to understand the difference between beauty bark and mulch. Beauty bark typically comes straight from nature, and often appears in larger chunks, while mulch may include recycled wood elements and is typically smaller or shredded. Overall, landscape bark tends to last longer than mulch in and around Tacoma, and retains more moisture for protective ground cover, making it an excellent choice for gardening and landscaping in the region.

Types of Beauty Bark in Tacoma


In addition to color and the type of wood, landscape bark in Tacoma is often categorized by size. Here’s a quick breakdown:

  • Mini nugget beauty bark. This type of bark is, true to the name, comprised of very small natural bark nuggets. It’s a great choice for small sections of landscaping and potted or raised plant beds.

  • Small landscape bark. An ideal choice for bordering drives or creating natural-looking walkways, this type of beauty bark has the added benefits of deterring weed growth and supplying more desirable plants with an extra boost of natural nutrients.

  • Medium beauty bark. As options among landscape bark in Tacoma go, this is one of our favorites. Visually striking and an excellent choice for a broad range of landscaping projects, medium-size beauty bark can also help provide an extra layer of soil protection from both heat and cold throughout the year.
